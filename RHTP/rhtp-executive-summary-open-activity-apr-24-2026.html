April 2026

Mid-Atlantic

  • Kentucky — Public Health Dental Hygiene Teams RFA remains one of the clearest live procurement paths, with the current public deadline landing today, April 24.
  • Maryland — April implementation materials continue to frame Maryland as near-term rather than fully live, but the state has now clearly anchored an April 28 pre-proposal conference and April/May competitive timing.
  • Tennessee — Funding Evaluation Consulting RFI remains recently closed after the April 17 deadline.
  • West Virginia — Program page still routes users to a live vendor/subrecipient portal, a public listserv, and webinar resources.

Northeast

  • Minnesota — April 24 review keeps provider-side RHTP funding active, while the HIT vendor RFP remains past due after the April 22 deadline.
  • Nebraska — April 24 review confirms multiple active RFAs remain in market and keeps the new RHTP 5.3 behavioral-health RFA in play. RPM due April 24; HAI due May 8; RHTP 5.3 due August 31.
  • New Jersey — Doula Integration remains the clearest public New Jersey package. Application deadline April 27.
  • North Dakota — April 22–24 activity remains one of the strongest late-month moves, with three new community/prevention opportunities and linked technical-assistance calls. New applications due May 22; Workforce Retention due April 30.
  • South Dakota — Digital health modernization and coordinated delivery RFP activity remains live through state posting channels.

Southeast

  • Florida — April 22–24 marks the largest new public move in market: the state announced more than $188 million in competitive RFA funding opportunities across six bundles and four RHTP regions.
  • South Carolina — Four Year 1 grant tracks remain live and unchanged in structure. Applications due June 1.
  • Georgia — April review still keeps the state in forecasted status but reaffirms named first-year categories including Telehealth Enhancements, Workforce Retention Technology, and Telepods.
  • North Carolina — Active public signal has shifted from the closed ROOTS Hub RFA to information sessions and public engagement materials.

West

  • Montana — State remains in active April RFP mode with Center of Excellence and implementation-partner procurement activity.
  • Nevada — Multiple live April funding paths remain open, led by the Flex Fund and Rural Tribal Health Transformation. Flex Fund due April 30; Tribal funding due May 29.
  • Oklahoma — Microgrants remain recently closed, but state roadshows and touchpoint webinars keep the market active and visible into June.
  • Oregon — Catalyst Awards remain live and the vendor interest path stays active. Proposals due May 26.
  • California — California’s April webinar and listening-session path continue to support early positioning even without a live public competitive package.
