What’s changed since last update

Updated to April 23, 2026. Material changes in this refresh: North Dakota expanded public activity on April 22 with three new community and prevention funding opportunities and scheduled technical-assistance calls; Nebraska added a new RHTP 5.3 behavioral-health RFA for equipment and facility enhancements; Minnesota remains Active for provider-side funding, but the HIT advisory services vendor RFP has moved past its April 22 deadline; Maryland remains forecasted, but April implementation slides now clearly show an April 28 pre-proposal conference and April/May competitive timing; and Oregon continues to show an active vendor-interest path tied to the live Catalyst Awards RFGP. Most other official state pages and procurement portals reviewed in this refresh did not show a clearer row-level change than the current master.

How to use this tracker

Click the State, Region, or Status column header to sort. Click a state name to open a concise popup with state RHTP information, active initiatives, public events, stakeholder or advisory signals, and vendor-related notes. Active means a live application or package-level RFA or RFP is open now. Recently closed means a recent cycle just ended but is still useful for partner mapping. Forecasted means an official state source has announced an upcoming window or release period. Monitor means the state is strategically relevant, but no public competitive dates were found on official RHTP pages as of the verification date.
