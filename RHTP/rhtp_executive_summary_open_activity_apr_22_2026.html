Immediate readout

Current open activity remains concentrated in the Northeast and West, with South Carolina and Florida standing out in the Southeast and Kentucky plus West Virginia providing the clearest Mid-Atlantic paths. Near-term technology fit remains strongest around telehealth, health IT, remote patient monitoring, interoperability, mobile care, and secure rural care environments.

July 2026

Northeast

  • Indiana — GROW Regional Grants stay in market beyond the LOI stage. Full applications due July 1. Strong regional hub opportunity for teleconsult systems, secure provider workflows, and digital-care buildouts.

Southeast

  • South Carolina — The current four-track grant cycle shows an anticipated notice of award on July 31, keeping the market active through the summer even after the June application deadline. Strongest fit remains telehealth, RPM, EHR, mobile access, and cyber-ready deployments.

June 2026

Southeast

  • South Carolina — Four Year 1 grant opportunities remain open across Connections to Care, Leveling Up, Wellness Within Reach, and Shoring Up to Sustainability. Applications due June 1. This is still one of the clearest and largest current technology-aligned targets in market.

May 2026

Northeast

  • Indiana — GROW Regional Grants remain open. Letters of Intent due May 15. Good opening for regional lead identification and early teaming.
  • Nebraska — Healthcare-Associated Infection Prevention RFA remains open. Applications due May 8. Good fit for clinical modernization and technology-enabled prevention environments.

West

  • Nevada — Rural Tribal Health Transformation funding remains open. Round 1 due May 29. Important tribal health pathway with infrastructure and service-transformation implications.
  • Oregon — Catalyst Awards RFGP remains open. Proposals due May 26. Strong fit for telehealth, IT modernization, cybersecurity, vendor partnering, and implementation-ready community projects.
  • Utah — State funding table still shows Year 1 opportunities with estimated openings beginning in May 2026. Use this as a pre-positioning state rather than a fully open state.

April 2026

Mid-Atlantic

  • Kentucky — Public Health Dental Hygiene Teams RFA remains open. Applications due April 24. Strong fit for telehealth-enabled public health teams, mobile workflows, and community-based access support.
  • West Virginia — Program page and vendor / subrecipient path are active now. Open signal is best used for grant-path monitoring, relationship mapping, and future package tracking.

Northeast

  • Minnesota — Health Information Technology Advisory Services RFP is open. Responses due April 22 at 4:30 p.m. Immediate vendor-facing health IT opportunity tied to RHTP subgrantee technology purchases.
  • Minnesota — MDH still shows open grant materials for rural hospitals, FQHCs, CCBHCs / CMHCs, and Tribal Nations on the RHTP funding page. Use this as an active provider-engagement lane alongside the vendor RFP.
  • Nebraska — Remote Patient Monitoring Implementation RFA is open. Applications due April 24. Direct fit for hospital-to-home RPM, connected devices, and virtual care workflows.
  • North Dakota — Workforce Retention funding is open for critical access hospitals and owned clinics. Applications due April 30. Strong early buyer-mapping opportunity ahead of broader mid-2026 releases.
  • New Jersey — Doula Integration in Rural New Jersey remains open. Applications due April 27. Good maternal-health engagement point, especially around workflow, access, and community care coordination.
  • Delaware — Initial RFP packages remain active through the state procurement path. Example live package due April 29. Strong fit for telehealth, RPM, and workflow modernization.
  • Ohio — Rural Health Transformation repository remains active and should be monitored continuously for package-level invitations and reference documents.
  • South Dakota — Digital health modernization and related RFP rounds remain live on the state posting board. Open signal is ongoing and package-specific.

Southeast

  • Florida — Vendor RFQs have been released through the state term contract vehicle. Open signal is immediate even where package-level deadlines vary. Strong fit for tele-specialties, tele-ICU, RPM, HIE onboarding, diagnostics, and cybersecurity-aligned support.

West

  • Montana — RHTP RFPs are posted and additional opportunities are expected. Open signal is current and package-specific.
  • Nevada — Rural Health System Flex Fund is open. Round 1 due April 30. Strong fit for capital equipment, telehealth environments, and rural infrastructure modernization.
  • Oregon — Catalyst Awards RFGP is open now, and Oregon is also actively collecting vendor interest forms to support local applicants and project teams.
