How this summary works

This summary is a change log, not a restatement of the full tracker. Older items stay in the tracker unless they had a new posting, release, closure, or verified status refinement in the month shown below. No future month sections are included.

April 2026

Mid-Atlantic

  • Kentucky — The Public Health Dental Hygiene Teams RFA moved into recently closed status after the April 24 due date.
  • Maryland — April implementation materials continue to frame Maryland as near-term rather than fully live, but the state now clearly anchors an April 28 pre-proposal conference and says program roll-out is expected in May/June 2026.
  • Tennessee — Tennessee’s public engagement path expanded with replay links now live for the March 31 and April 15 rural transformation webinars.
  • West Virginia — West Virginia published a new April FAQ package and continues to push the market toward a live listserv, advisory structure, engagement sessions, and coming competitive application activity.

Northeast

  • Delaware — Delaware remains one of the clearest early public procurement states, with the February RFP wave still standing as a visible market-entry path.
  • Indiana — GROW Regional Grants remain one of the strongest open public paths, with LOIs due May 1 and full regional submissions due July 1.
  • Minnesota — Provider-side RHTP funding remains active, while the HIT vendor RFP remains past due after the April 22 deadline. Final direct-allocation applications are due May 15.
  • Nebraska — Multiple active RFAs remain in market and the spring award wave continues. Additional RFA rounds are expected throughout the year.
  • New Jersey — Doula Integration remains the clearest public New Jersey package, with the application deadline on April 27.
  • North Dakota — April 22–27 activity remains one of the strongest late-month moves, with three new community/prevention opportunities, linked technical-assistance calls on May 4–6, and a new tribal liaison / rural advisor staffing update to support access to RHTP funding.
  • Ohio — Ohio continues to route public market visibility through its searchable solicitation repository and reference-document pages.
  • South Dakota — Digital health modernization and coordinated delivery RFP activity remains live through state channels following the March second-round release.

Southeast

  • Florida — April 14–22 remains the largest visible public move in market: Florida launched procurement opportunities on April 14 and announced more than $188 million in competitive RFA funding opportunities on April 22.
  • Georgia — Georgia moved from simple forecasted watch to a live stakeholder-engagement lane on April 22 with GREAT Health Advisory Council nominations.
  • North Carolina — Public signal remains anchored to the ROOTS Hub Lead path, town halls, information sessions, and a clear emphasis on hub selection and launch.
  • South Carolina — Four Year 1 grant tracks remain live and unchanged in structure. Applications due June 1; anticipated awards July 31.

West

  • California — California’s April 21 Spring 2026 webinar kept the state visible as an early engagement market even without a live competitive package.
  • Montana — Montana remains in active procurement mode around the Center of Excellence and implementation support path, with vendor resources and advisory materials still posted.
  • Nevada — Nevada still has multiple live public funding paths, led by the Flex Fund and Rural Tribal Health Transformation lanes.
  • Oklahoma — Oklahoma refreshed its dedicated RHTP funding page and materially expanded its public funding path with a live Doula Program RFI, a ROOTS school grant, and the Rural Regional Reorientation NOFO. May 8, May 25, and June 12 deadlines now anchor the market.
  • Oregon — Catalyst Awards remain live and the vendor-entry path stays active. The April 14 informational webinar materials remain posted on the official page.

March 2026

Mid-Atlantic

  • Kentucky — Public Health Dental Hygiene Teams RFA was released in March, creating the state’s clearest current public entry point.

Northeast

  • Indiana — GROW Regional Grants moved into active application status in March.
  • North Dakota — State announced its first RHTP funding opportunity in March focused on Workforce Retention.
  • South Dakota — The state announced a second round of RFPs focused on digital modernization, coordinated care, and project management.

Southeast

  • Louisiana — State launched a new Office of Rural Health Transformation and Sustainability and formal advisory structure in March.
  • South Carolina — March webinar materials previewed the grant application structure that opened in April.
  • Tennessee — Tennessee held its first public rural transformation webinar on March 31.

West

  • Arizona — Launch materials posted in March stated grant opportunities would begin posting in April and be fully posted by May.
  • Montana — State posted vendor webinar materials and kept stakeholder advisory structures visible as procurement began to move.
  • Nevada — First Flex Fund materials posted in late March, moving the state from planning into visible funding activity.
  • Oklahoma — March applicant webinar materials supported the now-closed microgrant cycle.

West

Mid-Atlantic

  • West Virginia — West Virginia announced its advisory panel for the Rural Health Transformation Program on February 27.
