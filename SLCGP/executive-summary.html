May 2026

Southeast

Georgia
Georgia remains one of the clearest active opportunities in the market. The official state page now shows the FY2024 application window open through May 15, 2026, which extends the usable sales window beyond the previously carried late-April date. Reps should prioritize qualified entities that can still assemble a timely application through Georgia EM Grants Manager.

Mid-Atlantic

Maryland
Maryland continues to show one of the clearest live participation paths. The state page says a second-round FY2023 application is open on a rolling basis and also references an additional short-term funding opportunity. This remains a strong state for immediate outreach where entities can move quickly on scoped cybersecurity projects.

Northeast

Pennsylvania
Pennsylvania still has no dated local application window on the Commonwealth page. The official guidance says local governments will participate in committee-identified projects through the Commonwealth and that more information for 2025 funding will be provided later. This remains a monitor-and-warm state rather than an open-state push.

West

Washington
Washington remains one of the strongest near-term opportunities. WaTech continues to advertise the next round as opening May 4, 2026 with applications due June 12, 2026, giving reps a defined runway for prospecting and pre-positioning. This is a good state for immediate outreach into likely local government, school, and public-sector technology buyers.
Oklahoma
Oklahoma still posts multiple 2026 deadlines, and the next actionable date after the April cycle is July 31, 2026. The page also says funding is not currently accessible and that additional funding is expected later in the year, so outreach should be framed as pipeline building rather than confirmed spend. Reps should stay engaged but avoid overstating immediate fund availability.

April 2026

Northeast

Wisconsin
Wisconsin remains closed for the last posted round, but the state is still in review and scoring for FFY23 round-two applications. The page says applicants should keep watching for the FFY24 NOFO release, and the federal shutdown is still affecting FEMA response. This remains a monitor state with future-cycle relevance.

West

New Mexico
New Mexico continues to accept participation interest and request forms for prospective SLCGP-funded services tied to State Fiscal Year 2027. The structure is service-based rather than a simple cash subaward model, which favors reps that can align to shared services or state-managed delivery. This is still a live strategic state for partnership conversations.
Idaho
Idaho remains active through Operation Cyber Idaho. The page still includes a ready-to-apply path for cities, counties, school districts, tribes, and community partners seeking SLCGP-funded services. This is a meaningful open-state signal and should stay in the active sales rotation.
Oregon
Oregon continues to say the funding opportunity is temporarily closed as of April 1, 2026, with new applications anticipated in Summer 2026. The message is clear enough for reps to stay warm but not position the state as open. Oregon stays in the upcoming pipeline rather than the active pipeline.

February 2026

Southeast

Mississippi
Mississippi's last clearly posted window opened January 15, 2026 and closed February 27, 2026. The state page also says Batch 4 participation has not yet been determined, leaving the next cycle uncertain. This is a watch state until a new opportunity is formally posted.

Northeast

Kansas
Kansas remains closed after its January 1 to February 28, 2026 submission window. The state page indicates Phase 2 applications are being reviewed by the State Cybersecurity Planning Committee before federal review and approval. This remains a post-submission monitoring state rather than a current application state.

January 2026

West

California
California remains closed based on its formal FFY2024 local and tribal RFP, which ran January 16 to March 13, 2026. It is still one of the cleaner documented competitive state models, but there is no newly posted open window at this time. This state should stay in the historical/reference set until a fresh release appears.
