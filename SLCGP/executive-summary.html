Open / closing states

Current live states and near-term closing states that deserve active outreach.
Southeast

Alabama

Alabama's official apply page says local government entities should apply online to participate in eligible projects and that projects are approved on a first-come, first-served basis. Approved projects provide one-time cybersecurity services rather than direct grant cash to local entities.

Mid-Atlantic

Maryland

Maryland's cyber preparedness funding page says a second-round application for FY2023 SLCGP funds is now open and applications will be accepted on a rolling basis. The page also continues to reference an additional funding opportunity for short-term projects.

West

Idaho

Idaho's Operation Cyber Idaho page includes an active 'Ready to Apply' path for cities, counties, school districts, tribes, and community partners to register for SLCGP-funded services, while program work is described as continuing through federal fiscal year 2025-2026.

West

New Mexico

New Mexico's SLCGP page says the Cybersecurity Planning Committee is accepting participation interest and request forms for cybersecurity services to be included in prospective projects for State Fiscal Year 2027. If approved by the Committee, the services will be offered free of charge using SLCGP funding on a first-come, first-served basis.

Southeast

Georgia

Georgia's GEMA/HS and GTA materials indicate the FY2024 SLCGP award opportunity opened April 1, 2026 and remains due May 15, 2026. GEMA/HS also announced the FY2025 SLCGP grant will open September 1, 2026.

Southeast

South Carolina

South Carolina's State Administrative Agency page links the updated 2024 SLCGP Application Proposal Worksheet, which says proposals are due by May 15, 2026. The page continues to route eligible entities through SLED for homeland security-related grant submissions.

West

Wyoming

Wyoming Homeland Security says the SLCGP application period is now open and all applications must be submitted via ZoomGrants no later than Friday, May 15, 2026. The page directs applicants to the SLCGP Information Sheet for additional details and the link to apply.

Northeast

Missouri

Missouri's FY2025 SLCGP NOFO says the application start date is April 13, 2026 and the application submission deadline is May 20, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. CST. The state's application workshop materials continue to point applicants to WebGrants and the supporting assessment tools.

West

Washington

Washington's WaTech page says the FY25 State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program competitive project solicitation is open. Applications are due June 12, 2026.

West

Oklahoma

Oklahoma's grants page says the State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program is accepting new applications and lists 2026 deadlines of January 30, April 30, July 31, and October 30. The page also says funding is not currently accessible and that additional funding is expected later this year.

Coming soon

States with credible next-cycle signals but no fully posted current local window.
Northeast

Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania's official cybersecurity grant page says local governments will be able to apply to the Commonwealth to participate in cybersecurity projects identified by the committee, cannot apply directly to the federal government, and should sign up for updates. The page says more information to apply for 2025 funding will be provided later.

West

Montana

Montana says the most recent general application window closed January 9, 2026 and that the next round will open by late spring 2026. The official page also shows a special application window for end-of-life network hardware replacement with a posted deadline of May 27, 2026.

West

Oregon

Oregon's official SLCGP page says the funding opportunity is temporarily closed as of April 1, 2026 and that new applications are anticipated in Summer 2026. The page continues to direct applicants to Oregon's Cybersecurity Plan and the SLCGP administrative email for questions.
