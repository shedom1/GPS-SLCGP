May 2026

West

Washington
Washington remains one of the clearest live opportunities in the market. The WaTech page still points to the round that opened May 4, 2026 and is due June 12, 2026, so the state remains in the active pipeline. Reps should continue prioritizing qualified public-sector prospects that can still move within the live window.
Montana
Montana should be treated as coming soon rather than a dated upcoming window. The official page says the next application window will open by late spring 2026, but it does not post a specific date. That means Montana belongs in the watch list until the state publishes a clear opening.
Oklahoma
Oklahoma still lists multiple 2026 deadlines and the next posted date after the April cycle is July 31, 2026. The page also says funding is not currently accessible and that additional funding is expected later this year, so the state remains a cautious pipeline play rather than a clean open-state push.

Southeast

Georgia
Georgia remains active and continues to show a hard application deadline of May 15, 2026 on the official state page. That keeps Georgia in the immediate-action group for public-sector outreach. Reps should stay focused on qualified entities that can still assemble a complete submission through Georgia EM Grants Manager.
South Carolina
South Carolina remains active and still shows proposals due May 15, 2026 through the updated SAA worksheet route. This is still a valid closing-window state for qualified public-sector prospects. It should stay in the near-term action group until the deadline passes.
Alabama
Alabama remains active through a first-come, first-served participation model. The state continues to route local government entities into eligible service projects rather than direct grant cash. This remains a live but service-structured opportunity.

Mid-Atlantic

Maryland
Maryland continues to show one of the clearest live participation paths. The state page still says a second-round FY2023 application is open on a rolling basis and references a short-term funding opportunity. This remains a strong state for immediate outreach where entities can move quickly on scoped cybersecurity projects.

Northeast

Missouri
Missouri remains open under its FY2025 SLCGP NOFO, with applications due May 20, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. CST. It remains one of the more clearly documented active windows in the market. Reps should keep Missouri in the closing-opportunity group until the deadline passes.
Pennsylvania
Pennsylvania still does not post a dated local application window. The Commonwealth continues to say local governments will participate in committee-identified projects and that more information for 2025 funding will be provided later. This remains a monitor-and-warm state, and Pennsylvania stays in the Northeast region throughout the tracker set.
