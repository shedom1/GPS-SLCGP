Montana

Do now Special window General round coming soon

SLCGP summary: Montana is a two-track campaign. The general round is still a coming-soon play, but a separate special application window is open now for end-of-life network hardware replacement.

Key datesSpecial hardware window closes May 27, 2026. General round four will open by late spring 2026.
Key requirementsHardware items must be end-of-life and unsupported after December 31, 2026. Partial awards may be made.
What is eligibleReplacement of end-of-life firewalls, network switches, and wireless access points for Montana local governments.
How to applyUse the MT DES special hardware application link on the SLCGP page and route questions to MTDESGrants@mt.gov.
Solutionz alignmentStrong fit for network refresh, secure wireless, switching, edge security, replacement planning, and implementation tied to unsupported hardware.
Who to targetCounty IT, county commissioners, city IT, school district technology leads, local government operators facing firewall/switch/AP refresh pressure.
Trigger questionsDo you have firewalls, switches, or access points that will be unsupported after 12/31/2026? Do you already have an inventory of devices that need replacement? Are you trying to use this hardware window or waiting for the next broader round?
Next actionWork hardware-refresh accounts now, especially counties and local governments with aging infrastructure, and prepare a broader follow-up list for the general late-spring round.
ChecklistConfirm unsupported hardware list; validate eligibility; review summary sheet; align scope; assign outreach owner; schedule follow-up before May 27.

West | Priority

Washington

Next 30 days Open State application

SLCGP summary: Washington remains one of the clearest active opportunities in the campaign. WaTech is showing the FY25 competitive project solicitation open now with a June 12 deadline.

Key datesApplication deadline is June 12, 2026.
Key requirementsProjects should align to Washington’s statewide strategic cybersecurity plan and follow the current competitive project solicitation process.
What is eligibleWashington’s current materials point to state, local, and Tribal governments as the target applicant groups for funding.
How to applyWashington directs interested applicants to the WaTech SLCGP page and the state application process; the current materials reference preparedness.grants@mil.wa.gov for interested governments.
Solutionz alignmentStrong fit for cybersecurity projects tied to state/local government needs: network security, endpoint protection, monitoring, identity, resilience, and implementation support.
Who to targetLocal governments, Tribal entities, state-adjacent public entities, IT directors, CIO offices, emergency management, and cyber leaders already working against statewide plan priorities.
Trigger questionsHave you already scoped a project for the FY25 solicitation? Is the project aligned to the statewide cybersecurity plan? Do you need help shaping a practical project narrative, equipment mix, or implementation path before June 12?
Next actionPrioritize Washington now as a live application state. Build a short-list of qualified public-sector entities with near-term readiness and move them into solution scoping before the June 12 close.
ChecklistConfirm eligibility; verify state/local/Tribal status; align project to state plan; define scope; assign rep; outreach before deadline; confirm application contact and timeline.

West | Priority

Oklahoma

Open Quarterly cycle Next posted deadline July 31

SLCGP summary: Oklahoma is still a valid campaign state, but reps should message it carefully. The state grants page says new applications are being accepted and lists quarterly 2026 deadlines, while also noting funding-access caveats.

Key datesCurrent 2026 deadlines listed by the state are January 30, April 30, July 31, and October 30. The next posted deadline is July 31, 2026.
Key requirementsProjects must be tied to the State Cyber Plan. Oklahoma says projects must be feasible, effective at reducing the identified risk, and able to be completed in the grant period of performance.
What is eligibleOklahoma’s posted announcement says eligible applicants are state and local units of government, publicly funded educational institutions, and tribal governments. Private and nonprofit entities are not eligible.
How to applyOklahoma says the complete application package must be emailed to hsgrants@okohs.ok.gov and reviewed by the Oklahoma Cybersecurity Planning Committee. Applicants are directed to review the state application announcement before submission.
Solutionz alignmentStrong fit for school districts, public-sector technology modernization, cyber hardening, and projects that can be tied directly to state cyber plan needs and implementation outcomes.
Who to targetLocal governments, tribal entities, publicly funded K-12 and higher education organizations, IT directors, emergency management, and public-sector cyber leaders.
Trigger questionsDo you have a project already tied to Oklahoma’s State Cyber Plan? Is the project feasible within the grant period? Do you need help packaging a solution, equipment list, or services scope for the next posted quarterly deadline?
Next actionUse Oklahoma as a structured pipeline state: qualify for the July 31 cycle now, but keep messaging precise because the state page also notes funding-access constraints.
ChecklistConfirm applicant type; verify public/tribal eligibility; map project to state cyber plan; review announcement; draft package; assign rep; set follow-up for July 31 cycle.
