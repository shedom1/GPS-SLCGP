April 2026

The April 27 refresh validated the late-April application picture across the states. The main theme is continuity rather than broad new openings: active states remain active, several watch states posted clearer operational language, and most unreleased states still do not show a new dated 2026 local window.

Southeast

Georgia
Georgia remains open through April 29, 2026 in Georgia EM Grants Manager for FY2024 SLCGP, and the state continues to point to a September 1, 2026 opening for FY2025.
Source
South Carolina
South Carolina continues to show a live FY2024 application cycle that started March 20, 2026 and runs through May 15, 2026, with applications submitted to SLED.
Source
Alabama
Alabama still operates on a first-come, first-served service-delivery model rather than a traditional local cash subaward model.
Source
Mississippi
Mississippi’s January 15 to February 27, 2026 batch is closed; the state still says any further Batch 4 opportunity has not yet been determined.
Source

Mid-Atlantic

Maryland
Maryland continues to show an open rolling second-round FY2023 opportunity together with an additional short-term funding opportunity.
Source
Virginia
Virginia continues to emphasize a committee-led shared-services model and directs entities to its VCPC meetings and VDEM listserv for future SLCGP notifications.
Source
Pennsylvania
Pennsylvania still directs local governments to the Commonwealth for committee-identified projects rather than a direct federal application path, with monitoring/sign-up language unchanged.
Source

Northeast

Michigan
Michigan now clearly states that FY2022, FY2023, and FY2024 awards are completed, while the FY2025 application date and submission deadline remain to be determined and Advisory Board review continues.
Source
Wisconsin
Wisconsin says FFY23 round-two applications closed January 30, 2026 and are under review; the state is telling users to watch for the FFY24 NOFO release.
Source
Nebraska
Nebraska’s site shows the 2024 subrecipient application closed and continues to post committee members and meeting listings, but no new dated 2026 local window is posted.
Source
Maine
Maine continues to show FY22 and FY23 participation windows closed, projects underway, and a partial-shutdown service pause affecting the KnowBe4 training component.
Source

West

Idaho
Idaho remains open via an active participation-interest model for prospective FY2027 shared services offered through Operation Cyber Idaho.
Source
Wyoming
Wyoming remains open; the public homepage says SLCGP applications are due May 15, 2026 through ZoomGrants.
Source
Oklahoma
Oklahoma continues to post quarterly 2026 deadlines, but the official page also says funding is not currently accessible and that additional funding is expected later this year.
Source
Oregon
Oregon remains temporarily closed as of April 1, 2026 and continues to say it anticipates opening for new applications in summer 2026.
Source
Washington
Washington still does not post a clearly dated 2026 local window; the state page continues to reference a November 2025 funding update and prior application webinar materials.
Source
