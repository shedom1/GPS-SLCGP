RUS DLT Match Calculator

Answer the practical sales questions: How much match do I need? How much grant can my current match support? What if I can match at a higher percent? Where can the applicant look for match money?

Match Inputs

Planning rule: FY2026 RUS DLT requires a minimum match equal to at least 15% of the grant amount requested. The grant request must generally be between $50,000 and $750,000. This calculator is for planning; final eligibility depends on USDA rules, documentation, and funding-source restrictions.

Question-Based Answers

How much match do I need?

Use the grant request. Minimum match equals grant request × 15%.

I only have this much match money.

Use the Available Match fields. The tool estimates the maximum grant request your current match can support.

I can match at this %.

Enter the percent. The tool compares it to the 15% minimum and estimates the grant level supported by your match at that rate.

Known project total?

Enter total project cost. The calculator estimates a grant/match split assuming the minimum 15% match of grant request.

Where Can Match Money Come From?

Potential sources to investigate: applicant cash; partner or end-user cash commitments; state/local funds; local foundation or philanthropic grants; eligible non-federal grant funds; Appalachian Regional Commission funds where applicable; and eligible in-kind items that are integral to the DLT project.
Watch-outs: E-rate funds are federal funds and cannot be used as DLT match. Vendor-donated match, discounts tied to later purchases, future revenue, already-installed equipment, and ineligible or non-integral items should not be relied on as match.
